About BeatBuddy

BeatBuddy is an advanced Discord bot that provides music and videos from YouTube to your voice channels.


Originally developed in Python as a simple, partially-functional bot in December of 2021, the bot was torn down and built from the ground up using Node.js and Discord.js from February to the end of March of 2022.


BeatBuddy features 24/7 activity and has a handful of both general commands and music specific commands. However, further development will be taking place in the near future in order to add:


  • Spotify and SoundCloud Support
  • A //scrub command that forwards to different points in the audio
  • A //skipto (int) command that skips to a specific song in the queue
  • More commands that return the output as a message embed
  • User-Suggested ideas or commands

So stay tuned for updates on the site and enjoy your time with BeatBuddy!

List of Commands

The prefix for all commands is "//".

For example, a user would enter "//hello" to use the greeting command.

General Commands

ping Responds with "pong"
hello (aliases: hey, hi) Says hey to the user who sent the command
clear int (aliases: delete, del) Clears the specified integer of messages up to 14 days old
help (aliases: h) Sends a list of available commands, disclaimers, and warnings

Music Related Commands

play [search or url] (aliases: p) Plays the requested song or playlist in the voice channel if nothing else is playing, otherwise adds the song or playlist to the queue
skip (aliases: next) Skips to the next song in the queue
pause Pauses the current song
resume (aliases: unpause) Resumes the song if paused
queue (aliases: q) Outputs the track number and title of each song in the queue
shuffle Shuffles the songs in the queue
songinfo (aliases: song, info) Outputs the title, artist, length, and date added of the currently playing song
join Joins the voice channel without audio until the play command is invoked
leave (aliases: stop) Leaves the voice channel and clears the song queue

Disclaimers:

At this time, BeatBuddy can only play music from YouTube links and searches. If a link from any audio provider besides YouTube is requested through the play command, BeatBuddy will not be able to find the audio and suggest that the user tries a YouTube link.
